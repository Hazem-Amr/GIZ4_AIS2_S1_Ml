Complete K-Means data preparation pipeline

Before you start: K-Means assumptions — check these first If your data violates these, no amount of preprocessing will fix bad results. Consider DBSCAN or GMM instead.
  • Clusters are roughly spherical (not elongated or arc-shaped)
  • Clusters have similar variance (not one tight, one spread)
  • Clusters have similar density (not one dense, one sparse)
  • You have a rough idea of K — K-Means cannot discover it automatically
1
Inspect
Histograms + boxplots for each feature. Note skewness, outliers, missing values, scales. This informs every step below.
↓
2
Handle missing values
Impute with median (skewed) or mean (normal). K-Means cannot operate on NaN — this step is mandatory.
↓
3
Encode categoricals
One-hot for nominal (city, gender), ordinal for ordered values. For heavy mixed data, prefer K-Prototypes over K-Means.
↓
4
Transform skewed features (conditionally)
Only apply log/log1p when: (a) the feature is right-skewed, (b) all values are positive, (c) the skew is actually hurting distance calculations.
⚠ Log transform is NOT always beneficial for clustering. Symmetric or bimodal features — skip it. Always re-check the distribution after transforming.
↓
5
Reduce dimensions / select features
In high dimensions, Euclidean distance becomes meaningless (curse of dimensionality). Two options:
Feature selection: drop irrelevant or redundant features. Correlated features distort distances by double-counting.
PCA: reduce to 2–10 components that explain ~90% variance. Removes noise and collinearity at once. Visualise clusters in 2D for free.
↓
6
Scale features
K-Means uses Euclidean distance — unscaled features with large ranges will dominate the result.
ScalerUse whenOutlier sensitivity
StandardScalerNormal distribution, few outliersHigh
MinMaxScalerNeed 0–1 range, no outliersVery high
RobustScalerOutliers present — uses median/IQRLow ✓ best default
↓
7
Choose optimal K — do not skip this
Elbow method: plot inertia (within-cluster sum of squares) vs K. Pick the K where the curve bends — adding more K beyond this gives diminishing returns.

Silhouette Score: ranges −1 to +1. Higher = better-separated clusters. Run both methods and confirm they agree. If they disagree, prefer Silhouette.
⚠ Never pick K by intuition alone. Always validate with at least one metric.
↓
8
Cluster
Use init="k-means++" and n_init=20 to escape local minima. K-Means++ gives better initial centroid placement than random.
↓
9
Evaluate clusters
Silhouette Score (higher = better) · Davies-Bouldin Index (lower = better) · Visual PCA or t-SNE plot.
If clusters look wrong — revisit step 4 (transform) or step 5 (features/PCA) before changing K.
